Group3 HW #1.1 — To-Do

UX evaluation of two government cultural websites through scenario-based user interviews.

🌐 Taiwan World Heritage Potential Sites 🏛 Ministry of Culture — Who We Are
Scenarios 2
Scenario 1
✈️
Foreign tourist planning a 3-day cultural trip
Browse the MOC English website for a general overview of Taiwan's cultural heritage, then switch to the Taiwan World Heritage Potential Sites website to find a site of interest (e.g., Kinmen Battlefield or Beinan Site), and evaluate if the information is sufficient to plan a concrete travel itinerary.
Scenario 2
📚
Student researching Taiwan's Cultural Preservation Policies
Find the detailed historical background of a specific site (e.g., Beinan Site) on the Taiwan World Heritage Potential Sites website, then search for relevant preservation laws or official announcements on the MOC website to support academic research.
Interview Questions 10 questions
Phase 1 — Warm-up & Behavioral Context
Q1When planning a deep cultural trip or looking for academic data, do you usually start with a search engine (like Google) or go directly to an official government website? Why?
Q2What are the most common frustrations you've experienced when using official government or cultural websites? (e.g., outdated info, "bureaucratic" interface, or lack of actionable steps?)
Phase 2 — Initial Visual & Brand Impression
Q3Upon opening these two websites, does the overall visual style (colors, layout) feel more like an "engaging travel brochure" or a "formal government archive"?
Q4Looking at the homepage menus, is it immediately clear to you where to find "attractions" versus "laws and regulations"?
Phase 3 — Feedback for Scenario 1 (Travel Planning)
Q5While browsing the potential sites, which elements (e.g., photos, historical stories, maps) were most effective in making you want to visit the site in person?
Q6When trying to turn a site into a "travel itinerary," do you feel the information is complete? Are there missing details like opening hours, ticket prices, or precise Google Maps links?
Phase 4 — Feedback for Scenario 2 (Academic Search)
Q7When switching from the Heritage site to the MOC site, did the transition feel seamless, or did it feel like using two completely unrelated systems?
Q8Regarding the MOC English site, do you find the descriptions easy to understand, or do they feel filled with difficult "officialese" or direct translations?
Phase 5 — Wrap-up & Future Improvements
Q9If these websites included an AI assistant to summarize "heavy regulations" or "long histories" into "TL;DR summaries" or "suggested routes," how helpful would that be to you?
Q10Based on today's experience, if you could change only one thing to improve your satisfaction, would it be the "interface design" or the "utility of the content"?
